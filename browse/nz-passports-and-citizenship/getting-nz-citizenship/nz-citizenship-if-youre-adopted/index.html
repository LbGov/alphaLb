NZ citizenship if you're adopted

If you were born and adopted in New Zealand you’re most likely a NZ citizen. If you were born or adopted overseas, you might be a NZ citizen, or you might be able to apply for NZ citizenship by descent.

Born and adopted in New Zealand

Born and adopted in New Zealand before 1978

You’re most likely a New Zealand citizen.

Born and adopted in New Zealand, the Cook Islands, Niue, or Tokelau between 1978 and 2005

You’re a New Zealand citizen. The exception to this is if your parents were diplomats from another country who were serving in New Zealand at the time of your birth.

Born and adopted in New Zealand from 2006 onwards

You’re a New Zealand citizen if you were born and adopted in New Zealand, the Cook Islands, Niue or Tokelau from 2006 onwards, and:

  • at least one of your adoptive parents is a NZ citizen, or
  • is entitled to live in NZ indefinitely.

You can use your birth certificate as proof of citizenship.

A person is ‘entitled to live in NZ indefinitely’ if they:

  • hold a NZ residence visa or permit, or
  • are an Australian citizen, or
  • hold a current Australian Resident Return Visa.

Born in New Zealand and adopted by parents who aren’t NZ citizens

If you were born in New Zealand and

  • at least one of your birth parents was a NZ citizen or
  • entitled to be in NZ indefinitely,

but you were adopted by parents who aren’t NZ citizens, you keep your NZ citizenship.

Born overseas and adopted in NZ

Born overseas and adopted in NZ before 1949

If you were born overseas and adopted in NZ before 1949, you can contact the Citizenship Office to check your citizenship status.

  • Freephone: 0800 22 51 51
  • Phone: +64 4 463 9361 (from outside NZ)
  • Email: citizenship@dia.govt.nz

Born overseas and adopted in NZ between 1949 and 1977

If your adoptive father was a New Zealand citizen

If you were born overseas between 1 January 1949 and 31 December 1977 and adopted in New Zealand, and your adoptive father was an NZ citizen, you can apply for NZ citizenship by descent. Your father must have been born in New Zealand, or been granted NZ citizenship before you were adopted.

If your mother was a New Zealand citizen

If you were born overseas between 1 January 1949 and 31 December 1969 and adopted in New Zealand, and your adoptive mother was an NZ citizen, you can apply for NZ citizenship by grant.

If you were born overseas between 1 January 1970 and 31 December 1977 and adopted in New Zealand, and your birth or adoptive mother was an NZ citizen, you can apply for either NZ citizenship by descent or a grant of citizenship. Your mother must have been born in New Zealand, or been granted NZ citizenship before you were adopted.

The difference between citizenship by grant and citizenship by descent is that a grant of citizenship can be passed on to your children if they are born overseas. NZ citizenship by descent cannot be passed on.

Apply for NZ citizenship by descent

Become a NZ citizen by grant

Born overseas and adopted in NZ between 1978 and 2005

If you were born overseas between 1 January 1978 and 31 December 2005 and:

  • adopted in NZ and
  • at least one of your adoptive parents was a NZ citizen (by birth or grant) at the time of your adoption

you can apply for New Zealand citizenship by descent.

Apply for NZ citizenship by descent

Born overseas and adopted in NZ from 2006 onwards

You are a NZ citizen if you were born overseas from 1 January 2006 and were:

  • adopted in New Zealand, and
  • at least one of your adoptive parents is a citizen or entitled to reside in NZ indefinitely.

You can use your NZ birth certificate as proof of citizenship.

A person is ‘entitled to live in NZ indefinitely’ if they:

  • hold a NZ residence visa or permit, or
  • are an Australian citizen, or
  • hold a current Australian Resident Return Visa.

Born and adopted overseas

Born and adopted overseas before 1978

If you were born and adopted overseas before 1978, you can contact the Citizenship Office to check your citizenship status.

  • Freephone: 0800 22 51 51
  • Phone: +64 4 463 9361 (from outside NZ)
  • Email: citizenship@dia.govt.nz

Born and adopted overseas from 1978 onwards

If you were born and adopted overseas on or after 1 January 1978, and at least one of your adoptive parents is a New Zealand citizen (by birth or by grant) you can apply for NZ citizenship by descent.

You are eligible if:

  • the adoption took place in a country which has signed up to the Hague Convention on Intercountry Adoption, and the adoption complies with those requirements, or
  • the adoption took place in a country where the laws around adoption in that country are considered to be compatible with NZ law, and either
    • the adoption took place before November 1992, or
    • you were under 14 at the time of the adoption.

Check with the Citizenship Office for further information.

  • Freephone: 0800 22 51 51
  • Phone: +64 4 463 9361 (from outside NZ)
  • Email: citizenship@dia.govt.nz

Countries that have signed up to the Hague Convention on Intercountry Adoption

If you were adopted by parents who aren’t NZ citizens

If you were adopted overseas by parents are NZ residents but not NZ citizens or permanent residents, and

  • the adoption is recognised under the Hague Convention, or
  • is recognised by the Adoption Act 1955

then you take on your adoptive parent’s residency status. Your parents need to apply for a visa for you as a dependent child.
