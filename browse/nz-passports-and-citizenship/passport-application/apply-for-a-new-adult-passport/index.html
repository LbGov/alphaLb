Apply online for a new adult passport

You need a passport to travel overseas. Most people can apply for a new passport online. It costs $180 and takes up to 10 working days.

You can apply online if you're 16 or over and have:

  • a verified RealMe account — this is a RealMe account where you have been to a NZ Post Shop and had your identify checked and a photo taken
  • a mobile phone that is linked to your RealMe account — you will need this to receive a security code to log in
  • NZ citizenship
  • a digital passport photograph
  • a credit card or a NZ Post Prezzy Card.

To apply, you need to answer some online questions before you can start your application.

Check you can apply online and start your application

What is RealMe?

You can't apply online if:

  • you've ever had a refugee travel document or certificate of identity travel document issued by the Department of Internal Affairs (DIA)
  • the name on your RealMe account doesn't match the name you want on your passport
  • you need an endorsement printed on the observation page of your passport because, for example, your full name has more than 48 characters or you're unable to sign your passport.

If you can't apply online

You can still apply for a new passport using the paper application form.

Application for a New Zealand passport - Adult (PDF 914KB) 

If you need to travel urgently

If you need to travel in a hurry you can apply online for an urgent passport, which is processed within 3 working days.

You can also get help in an emergency if you're travelling or living outside NZ.

If your passport is lost or stolen

You must report the loss or theft of your passport to the Passport Office so that your passport can be cancelled.

Lost or stolen passports

If you've changed your gender

If you're changing the gender on your passport, you need to complete the statutory declaration on your application form. You'll need to state which gender you want displayed in your passport and how long you've maintained this gender identity.

Changing sex or gender identity 

Documents you need

You don't need to send your old passport.

If you were born in NZ, you normally don't need to send any documents with your application.

If you’ve changed your name

If you’re changing the name on your passport, you need to send originals of your name change documents if:

  • you were born overseas and
    • registered your name change in NZ before 25 January 2009, or
    • changed your name overseas.
  • you had a Change of Name by Deed Poll Certificate in the UK.

If you’ve changed your name by marriage or civil union outside NZ, you need to send the original marriage certificate.

If you were born outside NZ

If you have NZ citizenship by grant or descent, you need to enter your citizenship certificate number on your application form. You don’t need to send the certificate.

If you were born in the Cook Islands, Niue or Tokelau, you need to send an original birth certificate.

Passport photos

If you’re applying for your adult passport online, you need to upload a digital photo.

You can check your photo online before you apply to make sure it meets the requirements.

Check your photo online

If you're using a paper application form

For paper applications, you need to:

  • supply 2 identical colour photographs
  • have your witness sign and date the back of one of the photos

Attach your photos:

  • to the sticky bit at the bottom of the form — if you're using a pre-printed (coloured) form, or
  • put them in an envelope or plastic bag and attach that to the form.

Do not staple, pin or glue your photos to the form.

Taking a photo

Get a photo taken by a professional

If you want to make sure your photo meets the requirements get it taken professionally.

You can do this at:

  • a pharmacy
  • a participating PostShop
  • a photographer or photo chain.

Take your own photo

Your photo must be taken directly from the camera — it can’t be scanned or edited in photo editing software.

The online system automatically checks your photo when you upload it, but it can still be rejected after your application is submitted if you haven't followed all the rules.

You can check your photo online before you apply to make sure it meets the requirements.

Check your photo online

The photo must:

  • be full colour
  • be no more than 6 months old
  • have your head centred, with a clear gap around the sides and top of the picture — your head, including your hair, must take up a maximum of 75% of the photo
  • not be altered in any way
  • be in focus, with no red eye, reflections or shadows on your face
  • be taken against a plain, light background
  • have strong contrast between your face and the background.

You must:

  • have your head straight on to the camera
  • have your eyes open and mouth closed
  • have a neutral expression — don’t smile or frown
  • make sure there’s no hair across your face or eyes
  • be able to see your eyes clearly through your glasses, with no reflections — you can’t wear glasses with heavy-rimmed or tinted frames or sunglasses
  • not be wearing any headbands or head coverings.

The file must be:

  • jpg or jpeg
  • between 500KB and 10MB
  • between 900px wide by 1200px high and 3500px wide by 6000px high
  • in 4:3 aspect ratio.

If you wear a head covering or head band, you can’t renew your passport online, so you’ll need a hard copy photo. Your face must be clearly visible.

Passport photo requirements

Taking a baby’s photo

The best way to get a picture for your baby is to lay them down on the floor on a plain sheet.

Your baby needs to:

  • be looking straight ahead
  • have a neutral expression
  • have their eyes open
  • be alone in the photo — it won’t be accepted if you can see any part of someone holding them.

When you need a witness

If you're applying online with a authenticated RealMe account, you don't need a witness to verify your identity.

If you're using a paper application form

You'll need a witness to:

  • complete and sign the witness section of the form — they need to include their NZ passport details
  • write your name on the back of 1 of your photos, then sign and date the back of the photo.

Your witness must:

  • be over 16
  • have a valid NZ passport
  • have known you for at least 1 year
  • not be related to you or be part of your extended family
  • not live in the same house as you.

Pay the fee

For an adult passport it costs:

● $180 for a standard passport (processed in up to 10 working days)

● $360 for an urgent passport (processed in up to 3 working days).

You can pay online by credit card.

If you're completing a paper application form you can pay by:

  • EFTPOST or cash — only if you go into a Passport office
  • cheque or money orders — payable to the 'Department of Internal Affairs'
  • credit cards — provide all the details in the payment section of the application form.

Fees and charges

Payment if you applied from overseas

You can apply for a NZ passport from overseas. You need to post or courier your form and documents to your nearest NZ passports office. There are offices in New Zealand, Australia or the UK. If your nearest office is in Australia or the UK, you must pay in the local currency (either Australian dollars or UK pounds).

If your passport is being sent to another country, you also need to pay a courier fee.

Fees and charges

When you get your new passport

Your new passport should be couriered to you within 10 working days. If you sent any original documents, they'll be returned with your new passport.

When your new passport arrives you need to sign it on page 3.
