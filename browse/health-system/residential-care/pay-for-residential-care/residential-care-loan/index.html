What you get

If you own your own home, you may be able to pay for your care by getting an interest-free government loan against the value of your property.

The government offers you a loan. Every 2 weeks the Ministry of Health pays your rest home or hospital for the cost of your care. Your loan increases by the same amount.

The loan only pays up to the maximum contribution — this is the cost of services which your rest home or hospital provides under their contract with the local District Health Board (DHB). If you need other services, you must pay for those yourself.

You can pay back the loan at any time, but it is usually repaid when your property is sold or when you die.

If you get a NZ government pension or another benefit

As well as the loan, if you receive NZ Super, a Veteran's Pension or another benefit, most of this payment goes towards the cost of your care in a rest home or hospital.

If you get NZ Super or a Veteran's Pension, you keep a personal allowance of $43.93 per week. You’ll also get an annual clothing allowance of $275.50 each April. The government reviews these allowances from time to time.

Who can get it

Work and Income can suggest that you apply for a Residential Care Loan if you’re not eligible for a Residential Care Subsidy.

Most people who get a Residential Care Loan are single or already have a partner in residential care.

To apply for a Residential Care Loan you must:

  • have been living in your own home before going into a rest home or hospital
  • own a home that puts you over the asset limit for the Residential Care Subsidy for example, your home is worth more than $224,654
  • not have other assets that add up to more than $15,000 or $30,000 if you have a partner.

Work and Income works out your assets and income using the same guidelines as for a Residential Care Subsidy.

Residential Care Subsidy — how to apply if you're 65 years or older

How you apply

You must first apply for the Residential Care Subsidy so your financial situation can be assessed.

If you’re interested in getting a loan, fill in the section of the Residential Care Subsidy form that relates to the Residential Care Loan. You need to provide your solicitor’s contact details.

After Work and Income has looked at your financial situation, they’ll tell you if they think you should apply for a Residential Care Loan.

How long it takes

Work and Income assesses your financial situation to decide if they’ll offer you a loan. It usually takes 6 to 8 weeks to set up the loan.

While you are waiting for a decision, you may have to pay for the cost of residential care yourself.

If your loan is approved, payments can be back-dated for up to 90 days from the date your application was received by Work and Income. You or your family may also be able to negotiate a suitable start date for the loan.

What happens next

If Work and Income approve a loan for you, they send a loan agreement to your solicitor.

You, or someone who has Power of Attorney for your affairs, signs and returns the agreement to Work and Income.

Enduring Power of Attorney — Protect your future

When you sign, you need to confirm that:

  • your rates are up to date
  • the house is insured.

A caveat or notice is put on the title deeds of your house until the loan is repaid. You pay all the costs of setting up the loan, eg your solicitor’s fees.

Tracking your loan

The Ministry of Health sends you a statement every 3 months so you know how much you’ve borrowed.

Moving to a Residential Care Subsidy

When the value of your home is reduced to the level where you would be eligible for the Residential Care Subsidy, the Residential Subsidy Unit at Work and Income write to tell you that you’re eligible for the Residential Care Subsidy.

Follow these steps to apply.

  1. Contact the Residential Subsidy Unit at Work and Income
    Freephone: 0800 999 727
    Email: msd_rcs@msd.govt.nz
  2. Ask for a review of your financial means — they send you the form.
  3. Complete the form and send it to:
    Residential Subsidy Unit
    Private Bag 9032
    Whangarei 0148

If the Residential Care Subsidy is approved, from then on the cost of your care that was paid for by the loan is covered by the subsidy. The loan stops and the Ministry of Health tells you the final amount that you owe.

Repaying your loan

You can repay the loan at any time. If it is paid by your estate, then it must be paid within 12 months of your death or when the property is sold — whichever is earlier.

The Ministry of Health (MoH) confirms the amount that must be repaid. The amount is calculated by Work and Income together with information they get from you about the sale of the property.

The amount you pay back can be less than the full amount of the loan as some of the estate costs can be deducted. These include:

  • real estate agent’s fees
  • solicitor’s fees
  • any equity in the house that you’re entitled to keep
  • a funeral allowance.

If your payment is late, the MoH can charge 10% interest per year.

Delaying repayment of your loan

You can delay paying your loan back, but you must:

  • owe more than $10,000
  • make an email request to Legal Services at the Ministry of Social Development — email msd.legal.executives@msd.govt.nz

The Ministry of Social Development and MoH look into your situation and decide if they'll let you delay the repayment. This usually happens when someone has died in residential care and their partner is still living in their joint home.
