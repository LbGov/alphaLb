Government help with housing if you're over 65

You can get help with rates, rent, board, your mortgage payments, ​repairs and maintenance. If your income is low, you can apply for cheaper housing to central government or sometimes your council.

Paying your rates

There are 2 main ways you can get help to pay your rates.

  • Apply for a rates rebate from your local council.
  • Contact your local council to find out if you can postpone or defer paying your rates.

The Accommodation Supplement can also help you pay your rates.​

Rates rebates

If you own your own home and you have a low income, you can apply to your local council for a rates rebate or refund. The most you can get is $610 a year.

Getting a rates rebate

Postpone or defer paying your rates

Some councils let you postpone paying your rates in some circumstances. Often they're paid back from your estate after your death. The rates plus interest and other costs come out of your estate.

Postpone or defer paying your rates

Rent, board, mortgage payments and rates​

You can apply  to Work and Income for the Accommodation Supplement.

Accommodation Supplement

You may be able to get the Accommodation Supplement from Work and Income if you can't pay your:

  • rent
  • board, or
  • mortgage and rates.

To apply, you need to be a NZ citizen or permanent resident.

Accommodation Supplement

You'll need to tell Work and Income about your income and assets.

What counts as income for benefits

You can't get the Accommodation Supplement if you're living in a house owned or managed by Housing New Zealand or a registered community housing provider.

Repairs and maintenance

You can only get help to maintain your home and garden if you:

  • need to fix something that's urgent or essential
  • have a disability or health problem.

You'll need to tell Work and Income about your income and assets.

Maintaining your house and garden if you have a disability or health concern

Low-cost housing

If you have a low income and can't find anywhere to live that you can afford, you can apply to the Ministry of Social Development for cheaper housing — sometimes called social housing.

Who can get social housing

Some local councils offer housing to older people for less than what you would pay to rent or buy on the open market.
