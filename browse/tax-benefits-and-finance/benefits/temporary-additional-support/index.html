Who can get it

If you can't pay for what you need you can apply to Work and Income for Temporary Additional Support. This is a weekly payment that can last for up to 13 weeks and you don't have to be on a benefit.

To get Temporary Additional Support you must be unable to pay your regular and essential living costs. These could include:

  • the cost of essential household furniture or appliances
  • employment costs such as:
  • childcare you need because you're working
  • travel to and from work.
  • health and disability costs that aren't covered by a Disability Allowance
  • phone costs if you need your phone for safety or security reasons, or for work.

You must also be:

  • 16 or older
  • a New Zealand citizen or permanent resident
  • living in NZ and planning to stay
  • not already getting the Special Benefit.

Temporary Additional Support is income and asset tested.

Cash asset limits

To be able to get Temporary Additional Support your savings and cash assets must be equal to or less than

  • $1,062.51 if you're single with no dependent children
  • $1,770.44 if you're married, in a civil union or de facto relationship, or a solo parent.

Cash assets are assets belonging to you and your partner that can easily be converted into cash. They include:

  • money in bank accounts
  • shares, stocks or bonds, including building society shares
  • investments or loans, including mortgage investments
  • bills of exchange or promissory notes — like an IOU
  • property or land that isn't used as your home
  • shares in any partnership.

Your car and personal effects that belong to you or your partner don't count as cash assets. Work and Income also don't include any caravan, boat or other vehicle that:

  • is worth less than $2,000, and
  • you or someone in your family is living in.

How to apply

It depends on your circumstances whether you can apply online or if you need to complete a paper form.

If you're already receiving payments from Work and Income

Download the Temporary Additional Support form from Work and Income or call them to ask for one.

Temporary Additional Support for existing clients (PDF 100KB)

If you're under 65, call freephone 0800 559 009 (NZ only)

If you're 65 or older, call freephone 0800 552 002 (NZ only)

Complete the form and return it to Work and Income. You'll need your Work and Income client number and to provide proof of your:

  • cash and non-cash assets — such as bank accounts, investments, caravans or holiday homes
  • accommodation costs — such as a rent book, tenancy agreement, mortgage repayments or rates bill
  • any costs related to you or a family member's disability — these need to be verified by a doctor
  • employment costs — such as public transport, childcare or phone costs (if you need it for work)
  • hire purchase or rental agreements for essential items — such as beds, fridges or washing machines.

If you're applying for a benefit or pension

If you're already applying online for a benefit or pension — such as Job Seeker, Sole Parent Support or NZ Superannuation — you can apply for Temporary Additional Support at the same time.

You need a RealMe account to apply online. If you don't have a RealMe account you can set one up. You'll need an email address and a cellphone to do this.

What is RealMe?

When you get to the Extra Help or Supplementary Assistance page in the online application form, answer 'Yes' when asked if you want to apply for Temporary Additional Support. You'll then be asked more questions relating to Temporary Additional Support.

Documents you need

You'll need to supply proof of your:

  • identity:
    • 1 form of government-issued ID with your full name and date of birth, such as your birth certificate, passport, driver licence or citizenship certificate
    • 2 other documents supporting your identity, such as your marriage certificate, phone or power bill, bank statements — 1 of these must be at least 2 years old.
  • IRD number — a letter or form from IRD
  • bank account
  • income — such as pay slips
  • name change (if any)
  • marriage or civil union
  • birth certificates for any children you have
  • income details for the last year — this needs to be the gross (before tax) amount.

You'll also need to provide proof of your:

  • cash and non-cash assets — such as bank accounts, investments, caravans or holiday homes
  • accommodation costs — such as a rent book, tenancy agreement, mortgage repayments or rates bill
  • any costs related to you or a family member's disability — these need to be verified by a doctor
  • proof of Working for Families tax credits if you get them
  • employment costs — such as public transport, childcare or phone costs (if you need it for work)
  • hire purchase or rental agreements for essential items — such as beds, fridges or washing machines.

What you get

If you're getting a benefit or pension

Work and Income use your income and costs to work out the shortfall between your income and your costs — your 'weekly deficiency'. The amount of Temporary Additional Support you get is based on this.

Formula for working out Temporary Additional Support

There is a limit to how much you can get as Temporary Additional Support. You'll get the smaller of:

  • the calculated 'weekly deficiency', or
  • 30% of your main benefit rate after tax.

For example, if you're getting the Sole Parent Support, the most you could get is 30% of $329.57 — an extra $98.87 a week. However, if your 'weekly deficiency' was worked out to be $75 — less than 30% of your main benefit — you would only receive that.

There are also limits on specific costs — for example, the most you could get towards a hire purchase repayment for a fridge is $23.94 a week.

Allowable cost limits

If you or a family member has a disability

You may be able to get a 'disability exception amount' if:

  • your 'weekly deficiency' is more than 30% of your main benefit, and
  • you or a family member is receiving the maximum Disability Allowance, and
  • you or your disabled family member has extra costs relating to their disability that are more than the amount they get from their Disability Allowance.

The extra costs can't be for counselling or transport to counselling.

If you get this, your Temporary Additional Support will be made up of:

  • 30% of your main benefit rate after tax, and
  • 30% of the total extra disability costs.

If you're not receiving a benefit

The most you'll get is 30% of the current Jobseeker Support rate that would be paid to you, and your partner, if you were eligible for it.

What happens next

After you've completed the form

You'll need to make an appointment with Work and Income. Call them and say 'apply' at the voice prompt.

If you're under 65, call freephone 0800 559 009 (NZ only).

If you're 65 or older, call freephone 0800 552 002 (NZ only).

At your appointment

Work and Income will want to know what you have done to:

  • increase your income, or
  • cut down your expenses.

Some examples of things you could have done include:

  • increasing the number of hours you work
  • looking for part-time or full-time work
  • getting budgeting advice
  • buying only essential items
  • growing vegetables or fruit
  • moving to cheaper accommodation
  • stopping your contributions to KiwiSaver
  • trying to pay off your debts by:
    • negotiating a lower rate of interest, or
    • consolidating your debts so the interest and repayment amounts are less.

Work and Income also check that you're receiving all the benefits you're entitled to, such as the Accommodation Supplement or Disability Allowance.

While you're getting Temporary Additional Support

You must let Work and Income know if:

  • you move house
  • any of your weekly costs change — including rent or mortgage payments
  • you start or finish work — part-time, casual or full-time
  • you start a business or become self-employed
  • you start a new relationship or you break up with your partner
  • the number of children in your care change
  • you plan to leave NZ
  • there are any changes to your cash assets.

Work and Income will review your Temporary Additional Support and may increase, reduce, suspend or cancel it, depending on the changes in your circumstances.

How long you can get it for

You'll only get Temporary Additional Support for 13 weeks. If you still need it at the end of 13 weeks, you must apply for it again. Work and Income send you a letter reminding you about this 9 weeks after you start receiving it.

You can't re-apply online. Call Work and Income to make an appointment.
