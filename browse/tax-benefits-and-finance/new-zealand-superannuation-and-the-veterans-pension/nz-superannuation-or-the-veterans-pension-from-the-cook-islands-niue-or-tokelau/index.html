Who can apply

If you're 65 or older, have previously lived in NZ and are now living in the Cook Islands, Niue or Tokelau, you may be able to get NZ Superannuation or the Veteran's Pension.

NZ Super

To get NZ Super you need to be:

  • 65 or older
  • a resident in the Cook Islands, Niue or Tokelau
  • have been resident and present in NZ for at least 10 years since you turned 20 — at least 5 of those years must have been since you turned 50.

Being 'resident and present' in NZ means you must have made your home in New Zealand and lawfully lived there on a permanent basis.

Veteran's Pension

To get the Veteran's Pension you need to:

  • be 65 or older
  • be a resident in the Cook Islands, Niue or Tokelau
  • have been resident and present in NZ for at least 10 years since you turned 20 — at least 5 of those years must have been since you turned 50
  • have served with the NZ Armed Forces, and
  • have qualifying operational service.

Check to see if your service qualifies.

List of Qualifying Service for the Veterans’ Support Act 2014 (PDF 125KB)

How much you get

The amount you get depends on:

  • whether you're married or single
  • if you're receiving a pension or benefit from another country, and
  • how long you lived in NZ for.

If you've lived in NZ for:

  • 20 or more years, you'll get the full rate for your situation
  • between 10 and 20 years, you'll get 5% of the basic rate for each year you've lived in NZ
  • 10 years, you'll get half the basic rate — that is, 50%.

How to apply

You can apply for NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension from the Cook Islands, Niue or Tokelau.

You should apply 2 to 3 months before you turn 65, or as soon as possible if you're already 65. You won't get back paid if you apply after you're 65.

NZ Super or Veteran’s Pension for Cook Islands, Niue and Tokelau residents

From the Cook Islands

You can get an application form:

  • from the New Zealand High Commission in Rarotonga
  • your outer-island administration office, or
  • by emailing International Services at Work and Income.

Email: international.services@msd.govt.nz

Once you've completed the form, contact the New Zealand High Commission or your nearest administration office to see if you need to make an appointment to bring the form and your documents in.

Factsheet in Cook Island Maori (PDF 374KB)

From Niue

You can get an application form:

  • from the New Zealand High Commission in Niue, or
  • by emailing International Services at Work and Income.

Email: international.services@msd.govt.nz

Once you've completed the form, contact the New Zealand High Commission in Alofi to see if you need to make an appointment to bring the form and your documents in.

Factsheet in English and Niuean (PDF 362KB)

From Tokelau

You can get an application form:

  • from the New Zealand High Commission in Apia, Samoa
  • from the Tokelau Apia Liaison Office
  • from the Tokelau official on your atoll, or
  • by emailing International Services at Work and Income.

Email: international.services@msd.govt.nz

Once you've completed the form, contact the NZ High Commission in Apia or your local official to see if you need to make an appointment to bring the form and your documents in.

Factsheet in Tokelauan (PDF 358KB)

Documents you'll need

You'll need to take in:

  • 1 form of government-issued ID — that has your full legal name and date of birth — such as your:
    • birth certificate
    • passport
    • driver licence
    • firearms licence
  • 2 more documents supporting your identity (one of these has to be at least 2 years old), like your:
    • marriage certificate
    • bank statement
    • phone or power bill with your name and address on it, and
    • proof of any name change such as a marriage certificate, deed poll or name change record.

You'll also need a bank statement or deposit slip showing your account name, number and the bank logo.

If you're married, in a civil union or have a partner

If your partner isn't receiving NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension, you'll need to bring proof of their identity.

What happens next

The High Commission or local office checks your form, copies your documents and sends your application to New Zealand to be processed. Your documents are given back to you.

Work and Income sends you a letter to let you know if you'll get NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension. You'll be paid every 4 weeks if your bank account is in the islands, or every 2 weeks if you have a NZ bank account.
