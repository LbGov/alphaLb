Including your partner

If your spouse or partner doesn't qualify for NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension — because they aren't 65 yet or they haven't lived in NZ for long enough — you can choose to include them in yours.

Work and Income call them a 'non-qualified partner'.

In 2017, if your combined income (not including your NZ Super) is equal to or more than $27,839.31 a year, it's not financially worthwhile to include your partner.

Your partner must be an:

  • NZ citizen
  • NZ permanent resident
  • Australian citizen, or
  • Australian permanent resident.

They must normally live in NZ and intend to stay here.

There may be exceptions to this if your partner is a refugee or in the process of applying for a residence visa. Talk to Work and Income about this.

Freephone: 0800 522 022 (NZ only)

Email: seniors@msd.govt.nz

What you'll get

If you include your partner, you'll both get NZ Super or Veteran's Pension payments at the 'couples with non‑qualified spouse included' rate. Your partner also receives a SuperGold Card.

What you can both earn

If you include your partner your NZ Super or Veteran's Pension becomes income tested. Any income the 2 of you earn (on top of your NZ Super or Veteran's Pension) may affect the amount of NZ Super or Veteran's Pension you get. Together, you and your partner can earn up to $100 a week (before tax) a week without affecting your payments.

Income includes:

  • wages or salary
  • interest from savings
  • returns from investments
  • rental income
  • dividends from shares
  • income from trusts.

If, between the 2 of you, you earn more than $100 a week (before tax), your NZ Super or Veteran's Pension will be cut by 70 cents for every dollar of income you earn over $100 (before tax).

Example

Total weekly gross (before tax) income $400.00
Less income allowed before NZ Super is affected  $100.00
Equals income to calculate deduction with $300.00
Amount to be taken away from NZ Super will be 70 cents per $1.00 of income over $100 ($300 X 0.7)  $210.00

Work and Income can help you work out if it is a good idea to include your partner or not.

Freephone: 0800 552 002 (NZ only)

Email: seniors@msd.govt.nz

How to include your partner

Your partner needs to download, print out and complete the Spouse or Partner form for either NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension — whichever you're getting. They need to supply identification and other documents, which are listed on the form.

If you're getting NZ Super

You also need to sign the form. Once your partner has completed the form, call Work and Income to make an appointment.

New Zealand Superannuation Spouse/Partner application form (PDF 124KB)

Freephone: 0800 552 002 (NZ only) 

Email: seniors@msd.govt.nz

If you're getting a Veteran's Pension

Once your partner has completed the form, it needs to be posted with certified copies of their documents to:

Veteran’s Pension Centre

PO Box 5515

Wellington 6145

Normally the Veteran's Pension team at Work and Income will follow up with a phone call.

Freephone: 0800 650 656 (NZ only)

Email: veteranspension@msd.govt.nz

Once your partner is included

You can decide to exclude your partner from your NZ Super at any time — for example, if one of you gets a new job that pays more.

Your partner should apply for NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension in their own right, up to 8 weeks before they turn 65 or meet the residency criteria.

Apply for NZ Superannuation

Veteran's Pension

As well as the normal obligations you have if you're getting payments from Work and Income, you must also let them know if you or your partner:

  • start or finish work
  • open a business
  • become self-employed
  • have any other changes to your income or finances.

What you have to do when you're getting a benefit

If one of you gets ACC weekly payments

If you or your non-qualified partner get weekly compensation payments from ACC, these are counted as income.

NZ Superannuation and ACC weekly compensation

If you travel or move overseas

If you and your partner want to travel overseas for more than 26 weeks or move to another country, you can apply to take some or all of your NZ Super or Veteran's Pension with you, but your non-qualifying partner can't.

Travelling overseas with NZ Super or Veteran's Pension

Moving overseas if you get NZ Super or Veteran's Pension

Long-term residential care

If you go into long-term care in a rest home or hospital, your non-qualifying partner's NZ Super stops. They can be moved onto an Emergency Benefit paid at the single rate of the Supported Living Payment. They may also be eligible for a Special Disability Allowance.

Emergency Benefit

Help for partners staying at home

Hospital stay if you get NZ Super

If you need to stay in hospital for more than 13 weeks, your NZ Super can be reduced to the hospital rate of $43.93 a week (after tax), unless you have a dependent child. Your partner's NZ Super can be increased to the 'single, living alone' rate, whether they are a non-qualifying partner or not.

Living alone on NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension

Hospital stay if you get the Veteran's Pension

If you need to stay in hospital for more than 13 weeks, your Veteran's Pension continues to be paid at the same rate.

However, if your partner is hospitalised for more than 13 weeks, their payments are reduced to the hospital rate after 13 weeks if you were:

  • assessed as having less than a 52% whole-body impairment, or
  • receiving less than 70% of the War Disablement Pension.

Work and Income can increase your rate to the 'single, living alone' rate.

Freephone: 0800 650 656 (NZ only)

Email: veteranspension@msd.govt.nz

Prison

If you're in prison, both you and your non-qualifying partner's NZ Super or Veteran's Pension stops. Work and Income may be able to move your partner to another benefit — they should contact Work and Income.

Freephone: 0800 552 002 (NZ only)

Email: seniors@msd.govt.nz
