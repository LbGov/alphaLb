Moving to a Pacific country

You can normally get some or all of your NZ Superannuation or Veteran's Pension if you move to a Pacific country to live.

You can get all or some of your NZ Super or Veteran's Pension if you're going to live in one of the 22 Pacific countries for longer than a year.

Pacific countries where you can keep getting NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension

  • American Samoa
  • Cook Islands
  • Federated States of Micronesia
  • Fiji
  • French Polynesia
  • Guam
  • Kiribati
  • Marshall Islands
  • Nauru
  • New Caledonia
  • Niue
  • Northern Mariana Islands
  • Palau
  • Papua New Guinea
  • Pitcairn Island
  • Samoa
  • Solomon Islands
  • Tokelau
  • Tonga
  • Tuvalu
  • Vanuatu
  • Wallis and Futuna

Who can apply

To apply, you must be either:

  • receiving NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension in your own right — you can't do this if you're only included in your partner's payments, or
  • entitled to receive NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension — so long as you meet the residency requirements without including any time spent in other countries.

Getting NZ Superannuation or the Veteran's Pension

You must apply while you're still living in New Zealand.

If you're a resident of the Cook Islands, Niue or Tokelau

Since 1 July 2015 you can apply for NZ Super or the Veteran's Pension from these islands.

Applying from the Cook Islands, Niue or Tokelau

How to apply

Download the Application for New Zealand Payment Overseas form and complete it. You'll also need to meet with Work and Income.

Contact International Services at Work and Income at least 6 weeks before you leave NZ to arrange a meeting.

Application for New Zealand Payment Overseas form (PDF 99.4KB)

Freephone: 0800 552 002 (NZ only)

Phone: +64 4 978 1180

Email: international.services@msd.govt.nz

At the meeting you'll need to have:

  • your completed application form
  • your passport
  • another form of ID, such as your driver licence
  • your tickets or itinerary.

How much you'll get

It depends how many years you lived in New Zealand after the age of 20, and whether you're eligible for any social security pension from another country — any money you receive from another country will be taken off your NZ Super.

If you've lived in NZ for:

  • 20 or more years, you'll get the full rate
  • between 10 and 20 years, you'll get 5% of the basic rate for each year you've lived in NZ
  • 10 years, you'll get half the basic rate — that is 50%.

You can't get the 'single, living alone' rate if you're living outside of NZ — instead you'll receive the 'single sharing' rate, which is slightly less.

Superannuation payment rates

Getting paid

If you're living in one of these Pacific countries, your NZ Super or Veteran's Pension is paid to you at the gross (before tax) rate. You can choose to have it paid:

  • every 4 weeks into an overseas bank account, or
  • every 2 weeks into a New Zealand bank account.

If you have an overseas account or get paid in overseas dollars, the amount you get each pay varies depending on the exchange rate.

Residing in certain Pacific countries

Paying tax on your NZ Super or Veteran's Pension

You may need to pay tax — it depends on the tax laws of the country you're living in. Contact their tax department to find out.

If your NZ Super or Veteran's Pension is being paid into a New Zealand bank account you may need to pay tax on it. Check with IRD.

Individual income tax — living or going overseas

Once you're living overseas

You need to let Work and Income know if anything changes, such as your:

  • address,
  • income, or
  • relationship.

Work and Income sends you a declaration every 6 or 12 months depending on your circumstances. The declaration is to check if anything has changed, such as your relationship status or your contact details. You must complete and return it.

Your NZ Super or Veteran's Pension stops if the declaration isn't returned within 8 weeks and is cancelled if it's not returned within 16 weeks.
