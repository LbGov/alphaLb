What do you think is the biggest contributing factor to a dog attack?

Education about dog behaviour

Please explain why this is the biggest contributing factor:

Not only do the people who choose to own a dog need to be educated about how to properly care for, train and read your dogs behavior, but the public, especially children, need the knowledge of how to be safe and safely act around dogs. The owner has the responsibility to provide adequate training for the dog, especially if the dog will be in public situations such as beaches. However from experience, a child as well as other adults, need to learn how to approach and safely act around a dog.

Require owners to complete dog obedience classes, Education about dog behaviour

Better education is a must for dog owners and children especially. Dog owners need to be responsible, maybe for every dog adopted or purchased, it could be compulsory to attend a training course, and if not completed you cannot own the dog. Being a dog owner who loves animals, I think it is up to the owner to provide the necessary training for the dog and if they don't have that skill, then they need to be educated before owning a dog.

I have: (select all that apply)

a dog

What do you think is the biggest contributing factor to a dog attack?

Education about dog behaviour

Please explain why this is the biggest contributing factor:

I think dog owners and parents/the other party are big factors too, but ultimately the thing that is going to allow an accident to occur is the lack of education of either/both parties. If both know how to deal with a growling dog, and recognise that as a warning and that the dog is uncomfortable but doesn't WANT to attack, they will not carry out behaviour that pushes the dog over the brink and makes it think its only option is to attack. A dog that feels threatened will try to protect itself, but giving a warning is actually a helpful thing and people should be able to recognise that it does not have to be a precursor to an attack, but rather the thing that helps you avoid one.

Anonymous | 15 August 2016

I have: (select all that apply)

a dog

What do you think is the biggest contributing factor to a dog attack?

Education about dog behaviour

Please explain why this is the biggest contributing factor:

Responsible dog ownership (including properly containing, identifying, registering, desexing, and providing appropriate and adequate socialisation and training for your dog) is key to reducing the risk of dog bites.

What do you think is the best way to reduce dog attacks? (select all that apply)

Education about dog behaviour

How do you think dog owners can help to reduce attacks? (select all that apply)

Other

If you selected 'Other', please explain how dog owners can reduce attacks.

With the exception of muzzling in public, all of the above. Breed Specific Legislation (BSL) does not reduce dog bites. BSL involves targeting and banning certain dog breeds, and evidence shows that it is not effective. Wherever BSL has been implemented, it has not been shown to reduce public risk and is likely to create even more risk of harm from dogs, due to the incorrect belief that dogs other than those that have been banned are ‘safe’.

How do you think councils can help to reduce dog attacks? (select all that apply)

Other

If you selected 'Other', please explain how councils can reduce attacks.

Education about dog behaviour, and how to properly read dogs’ body language, approach and react to dogs is important for all members of the public of all ages, whether they own dogs or not. The majority of dog bite incidents involve children under the age of 15, so educating children about how to safely interact with dogs and prevent bite incidents is particularly important.

I think the government can help to reduce attacks by: (select all that apply)

Increasing public awareness about safety around dogs

If you selected 'Other', please explain how government can reduce attacks.

Education about dog behaviour, and how to properly read dogs’ body language, approach and react to dogs is important for all members of the public of all ages, whether they own dogs or not. The majority of dog bite incidents involve children under the age of 15, so educating children about how to safely interact with dogs and prevent bite incidents is particularly important.
