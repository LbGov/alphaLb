Sue Adams | 2 August 2016

I have: (select all that apply)

a dog, concerns about dog attacks

What do you think is the biggest contributing factor to a dog attack?

Education about dog behaviour

Please explain why this is the biggest contributing factor:

Many people adopt dogs for the wrong reasons... ie. they're cute puppies, their partner/child wants one, they want a guard dog etc. - however, many have never owned dogs therefore have no idea how to train/treat a dog. They tend to treat the dogs as people, expect them to understand without training..... frustration for dog and person both

What do you think is the best way to reduce dog attacks? (select all that apply)

Require owners to complete dog obedience classes, Education about dog behaviour, Other

If you selected 'Other' as the best way to reduce dog attacks, please explain.

Require that people prove their capability as a dog owner. Not just for so called dangerous breeds, but all dogs. (Yes... even chihuahua owners)

How do you think dog owners can help to reduce attacks? (select all that apply)

Keep dogs on leash when in public , Be more considerate of other people and dogs in public, Properly fencing off property

How do you think councils can help to reduce dog attacks? (select all that apply)

Establishing more spaces for exercising dogs , Quicker responses to complaints , Punishing irresponsible dog owners

I think the government can help to reduce attacks by: (select all that apply)

Increasing public awareness about safety around dogs, Requiring dog owner licencing

Anonymous | 2 August 2016

I have: (select all that apply)

a dog, concerns about dog attacks

What do you think is the biggest contributing factor to a dog attack?

Dog owner

Please explain why this is the biggest contributing factor:

A lot of dogs are purchased for the incorrect reasons, eg guarding, fluffy toys. They are not trained correctly and sometimes are left chained & bored in the back yard. There is a need to register the ownership of dogs and breeders, and with the help of the veterinary associations make it cheaper to neuter & spay dogs up to a certain age. Banning dogs of certain breeds is alright, however a lot of people have no idea which breed they are looking at & presume pit bull at the slightest growl.

What do you think is the best way to reduce dog attacks? (select all that apply)

Require owners to complete dog obedience classes, Education about dog behaviour

How do you think dog owners can help to reduce attacks? (select all that apply)

Keep dogs on leash when in public , Owners and dogs must take obedience training , Be more considerate of other people and dogs in public, Properly fencing off property

How do you think councils can help to reduce dog attacks? (select all that apply)

Establishing more spaces for exercising dogs

I think the government can help to reduce attacks by: (select all that apply)

Increasing public awareness about safety around dogs, Requiring dog owner licencing

Dog owner

Any dog can be managed by the owner taking appropriate precautions, but any dog can be made dangerous in the wrong hands

Other

License dog owners. Have large fines to prevent people owning dogs without an appropriate licence. Remove owners license or require dog education training as required (trackable through the owner licensing system)

Owners and dogs must take obedience training , Be more considerate of other people and dogs in public, Properly fencing off property
