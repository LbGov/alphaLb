Catriona Ferguson

Submitted on 31/08/2016 2:07pm

What sector do you work in?

Cultural Sector

How often do you interact with the National Library of New Zealand?

Frequently

Do you think the 3 broad themes outlined (One knowledge network, A nation of readers, Words as taonga) are the right areas of focus for the National Library looking towards 2030? 

Yes

Why or why not?

They appear to be relevant both for the issues New Zealand is currently facing and address at least some of the issues which as a nation we are likely to face in the future.

Do the strategic directions fit with your view of the role of a national library?

Yes

Why or why not?

The strategic priorities appear to be forward thinking and innovative. They are relevant to New Zealand society and provide leadership both to the library sector and beyond that. As an arts organisation with a focus on increasing readership throughout New Zealand we are particularly delighted to see the focus on creating a nation of readers. Additionally it appears that the idea of the one knowledge network could benefit school libraries, a key tool for promoting NZ books and authors.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'One knowledge network' do you consider to be most important?

Local government & schools taking part in a shared library system

Why?

There is little information available on the readership of New Zealand books. Working across the sector, The Book Council has recently attempted to gather what data is available but existing systems are only able to provide a sketchy picture. It would be incredibly helpful to writers and others working in this area to be able to gather this information and use it for planning future initiatives and programmes.

Which of the proposed 'One knowledge network' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Significantly scaled up digitisation of NZ content

Why?

I am curious to know more about how the rights-holders of the work to be digitised would be compensated.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'A nation of readers' do you consider to be most important?

National reading strategy based on successful literacy programmes

Why?

A number of the suggested initiatives are likely to be of benefit to increasing readership throughout New Zealand. However a strategy to increase reading throughout New Zealand is vital to ensure that organisations are working in partnership towards agreed goals and to avoid duplication of effort and resources. Additionally, any strategy would ideally go beyond only looking at literacy programmes; the link between reading for pleasure and literacy is strong but there are many other initiatives and organisations who are working in the area of reading development but not strictly focused on literacy. These are potentially key partners in the development of a national reading strategy.

Which of the proposed 'A nation of readers' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Programmes to inspire reading & support literacy

Why?

I agree that we do need these programmes, however ideally they should be created and developed within the context of a national reading strategy in order to achieve maximum outcomes. A range of one-off programmes outside of a sector wide strategy are likely to be less successful than a more carefully thought through and joined up approach.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'Words as taonga' do you consider to be most important?

A greater diversity of people supported & inspired to create & share our diverse heritage

Why?

Given the diverse make-up of New Zealand's population, an inclusive approach to engaging with a range of different heritages would be extremely valuable.

Which of the proposed 'Words as taonga' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

A sustainable, fit-for-purpose digital preservation approach to manage new digital content

Why?

Without clarity on how creatives and right holders are considered I would be less comfortable with this.

What do you think is missing from the National Library’s draft strategic directions?

I would like to see more detail about cross-sector partnerships and also on how writers, publishers etc. are able to engage with plans to digitise content.

How does the National Library’s draft strategic directions fit with your organisation’s priorities?

The Book Council's goal is to encourage a strong reading culture in New Zealand with a particular focus on promoting our local writers. A Nation of Readers therefore aligns strongly with our strategic priorities.

In which areas are you or your organisation interested in partnering with or contributing to the National Library?

The Nation of Readers programme.

How would you like to be involved as the strategic directions are progressed?

As a potential partner

Any other thoughts you'd like to share?

Submitted on 31/08/2016 2:32pm

What sector do you work in?

National Library staff

How often do you interact with the National Library of New Zealand?

Frequently

Do you think the 3 broad themes outlined (One knowledge network, A nation of readers, Words as taonga) are the right areas of focus for the National Library looking towards 2030? 

Yes

Why or why not?

Broadly speaking yes, however we believe that there are opportunities where themes could be expanded on. 1. One knowledge network could be expanded to include reference to digital literacy and the development of the skills required to ensure all New Zealanders are able to engage with the “One knowledge network.” 2. A nation of readers. We feel that this is a very important area to focus on, and we have a role to play as a thought leader and connector with other partners in this field. 3. Words as taonga. We treat all of the taonga that is in our care with the upmost respect. There is a question around how taonga is defined. Whilst we appreciate the discussion that this term instigates, without facilitated discussion this may appear too limiting. Other suggestions could be stories as taonga or documentary heritage as taonga to better encompass the breadth of what is entrusted into our care.

Do the strategic directions fit with your view of the role of a national library?

Yes

Why or why not?

The Services to Schools Online Team believe that the National Library is well placed to provide leadership in all areas of the strategic directions. We are in a position to support the education sector by building skills and providing access to resources and supporting the development of new resources to inspire the hearts and minds of New Zealanders.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'One knowledge network' do you consider to be most important?

Significantly scaled up digitisation of NZ content

Why?

As a team we believe that the most important aspect is to have equitable access to the resources of the National Library. While we do think that digitization of content should be scaled up, it is as important that people have the requisite skills to access and use that material and that the content is framed in such a way that is useful and inspires curiosity.

Which of the proposed 'One knowledge network' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

National contracts for licensing e-content and new systems

Why?

Consolidated licensing e-content and systems to enable universal access through all public and school libraries. There is also the issue of equitable access relating to the one knowledge network, there needs to be a way to ensure that New Zealanders have digital literacy skills to engage with the one knowledge network.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'A nation of readers' do you consider to be most important?

A cross-sector partnership to improve literacy levels

Why?

Maintain a leadership role across the government, education and library sectors to raise New Zealanders’ literacy levels. We would like to see that leadership reflect a targeted approach that focuses on priority groups that have a high proportion of low literacy levels, including those born outside of New Zealand. This could be done by building upon knowledge that is already present within the multicultural diversity that we have in New Zealand. We also support the establishment of a Children’s Laureate. This high profile role has the potential to focus attention on the importance of reading.

Which of the proposed 'A nation of readers' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Public library membership from birth & early childhood

Why?

Public library membership from birth. This is not part of our role.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'Words as taonga' do you consider to be most important?

Digitisation and distribution of te reo Māori published content

Why?

Draw on the National Library’s unique collection and published Māori language collections to help revitalise te reo Māori. Collaborative technologies and digital communications can support new ways of working and sharing knowledge across cultural and heritage institutions.

Which of the proposed 'Words as taonga' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

A state-of-the-art National Library space for discussion & debate

Why?

Location of physical items and the experiences that go with that is not available for the vast majority of the population, creating equity issues. Most of the new public programs are based in in Wellington despite the fact that this is not the home of the largest proportion of the population. How can we make it possible for people outside of Wellington to see and engage with the Treaty of Waitangi Te Tiriti o Waitangi, the Women’s Suffrage petition and the Declaration of Independence?

What do you think is missing from the National Library’s draft strategic directions?

How the three themes fit together. Each theme has the ability to support the other two areas, what is the overarching goal if we progress each of these focus areas. A lens on digital literacy to help mitigate equity issues relating to the one knowledge network. Ways to encourage and inspire our customers to also be inquirers. We also would like to see recognition that our services must be fit for purpose, to meet the changing needs of the increasingly diverse New Zealand population.

How does the National Library’s draft strategic directions fit with your organisation’s priorities?

As part of the National Library they will eventually direct our teams priorities.

In which areas are you or your organisation interested in partnering with or contributing to the National Library?

Do you think the 3 broad themes outlined (One knowledge network, A nation of readers, Words as taonga) are the right areas of focus for the National Library looking towards 2030?

Why or why not?

None of the 3 are goals that NLNZ can deliver on it's own. They require partnership and leadership with other government agencies, NGO's, the education and private sectors.

Do the strategic directions fit with your view of the role of a national library?

No

Why or why not?

The National Library Act defines the purpose of NLNZ to enrich the cultural & economic life of NZ, including by collecting, preserving and protecting documents and making them accessible. A knowledge network that connects NZ libraries may meet this purpose but a nation of readers is an overreach

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'One knowledge network' do you consider to be most important?

National contracts for licensing e-content & new systems

Why?

Without fully and appropriately recognizing the rights of authors and publishers who's work NLNZ is wanting to make available to the entire country NLNZ will fail in it's duty to enrich the economic life of all NZers

Which of the proposed 'One knowledge network' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Local government and schools taking part in a NZ-wide shared library system

Why?

Each school in NZ is an individual entity that may or may not have a designated librarian. Engaging with all schools requires conversations and support from over 2500 organisations. Local govt engagement would be in addition to this but is possibly a more achievable target

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'A nation of readers' do you consider to be most important?

Programmes to inspire reading & support literacy

Why?

We have chosen this option as it is not clear what cross-sector means with some of the other options (between libraries or between a wider group of organisations?). High literacy levels are well known as an economic driver and are critical to NZ's future prosperity

Which of the proposed 'A nation of readers' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Distribute te reo material through e-devices to te reo Māori learners

Why?

There are significant funding and equity issues associated with this idea

Why?

Preservation is a fundamental role for NLNZ and should be the priority

Which of the proposed 'Words as taonga' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Digitisation and distribution of te reo Māori published content

Why?

Appropriate permissions for and recognition of iwi traditional knowledge will require a high level of dedicated resource with which to commence and sustain a project such as this

What do you think is missing from the National Library’s draft strategic directions?

A focus on the core functions of NLNZ in collection and preservation

How does the National Library’s draft strategic directions fit with your organisation’s priorities?

Part of CLNZ's role is to ensure that the legal rights of authors and publishers are respected and that they are appropriately remunerated for the use of the creative work. It is reassuring to see acknowledgement of licensing as a means of access to creative work, included in the strategy.

Submitted on 31/08/2016 3:00pm

What sector do you work in?

Library

How often do you interact with the National Library of New Zealand?

Frequently

Do you think the 3 broad themes outlined (One knowledge network, A nation of readers, Words as taonga) are the right areas of focus for the National Library looking towards 2030? 

Yes

Why or why not?

We don’t think that there is anything that we see the Library doing in the next 15 years that does not fit into either one or another of these categories.

Do the strategic directions fit with your view of the role of a national library?

Yes

Why or why not?

We feel that these broad directions fit with what we think that the National Library does for New Zealand. Generally, there could also have been more emphasis on collecting and supporting scholarship relating to NZ History.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'One knowledge network' do you consider to be most important?

Redeveloped digital services for better interaction with National Library collections

Why?

Better access to and easier reuse of the Alexander Turnbull Library and National Library collections is something that we feel that the Library as a whole has to constantly work towards and invest in. The definition of ‘better’ moves on with each achievement that we make in this area and we need to constantly reconfigure to meet clients expectations. It is heartening to see this idea so prominent, because we believe that it is important that clients can easily and directly access our collections without compromising the care for collections that our clients expect of the National Library. We are happy to see NLNZ/ATL making a commitment to access. A much easier online service will also help us offer more targeted services to our onsite clients, who can have quite different needs to online users. Throughout, we want to ensure that people always have a place to come to ask for help and expertise, whether online or in person.

Which of the proposed 'One knowledge network' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Local government and schools taking part in a NZ-wide shared library system

Why?

The type of demands that we get tell us that clients want the option of more services directly to them from the National Library, not always through public or university libraries. Also, this reads as over-confident to us. Does the National Library have the power to deliver in this area?

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'A nation of readers' do you consider to be most important?

A cross-sector partnership to improve literacy levels

Why?

We agree that literacy is one of the most important tools that you can have. A literate population that understands where it has come from is well positioned for the future.

Which of the proposed 'A nation of readers' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Public library membership from birth & early childhood

Why?

Maybe it would be more fruitful to look at the barriers that families experience to using libraries and assist with designing out these barriers out of our public libraries. Perhaps a public library charter for NZ?

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'Words as taonga' do you consider to be most important?

Digitisation and distribution of te reo Māori published content

Why?

It is wonderful for the Library to commit to promoting this particular, special type of content. The breadth of our collections and our commitment to being even handed can sometimes get in the way of achieving significant gains for our Treaty partners. Why limit this to published te reo content? There are more collections of letters and other unpublished material that Māori scholars should have easier access to.

Which of the proposed 'Words as taonga' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

A state-of-the-art National Library space for discussion & debate

Why?

The National Library building in Molesworth street was designed to have an auditorium. A well- built and well used auditorium was on the site for many years. The DIA as a whole need a space like this internally so that large groups can meet at once. The Public Programmes and He Tohu offerings at the Library need to be able to present to more than 80 people in one go. Conferences here would benefit enormously from this resource. But it seems an ill fit for a strategic document like this. This is an operational decision that should be well off the blocks by now, and should not need to ‘compete’ in this strategic space!

What do you think is missing from the National Library’s draft strategic directions?

We want to make a special plea for more communication support. We feel that our ability to get our message across to our users drives our success or failure. Recent repository projects have not prioritised intuitive or easy use by our clients. We would also like to see more support for an understanding of what it is that the National Library and Alexander Turnbull Library do. However, perhaps better services directly to clients will make promoting what we do less difficult?

How does the National Library’s draft strategic directions fit with your organisation’s priorities?

An enormous priority for us is streamlining and opening up our collections for legal reuse. This is present in the strategic direction but not a number 1 priority as we are experiencing it.

In which areas are you or your organisation interested in partnering with or contributing to the National Library?

As an integral part of it!

How would you like to be involved as the strategic directions are progressed?

Keep me informed

Any other thoughts you'd like to share?

We feel that care for NZ’s heritage is missing or dumbed down in this work. This is a major strength for us. We should be bringing it more to the front – in 15 years people will want to know how well we did at collecting the 90s/2000s digital output, for instance. Likewise, as we digitise more content, we can’t lose sight of the emotional and educational importance of analogue material to our users. We are happy to see signs of investment in core services. We feel that although many of our services that clients use are developed to be iterative, we do not adequately provision these areas to actually iterate once their phase as a project is over. Projects should not finish, they should turn into programmes. We want to listen to the client more – surveys and analysis of our online users, and more opportunities to test online material directly with clients before we deploy. Librarians have a role as educators – we value this work – if we invest in less procedural work on the front line of customer service, this would mean more talks, publications, research in partnership with universities, other libraries and independent researchers.

It is a shame the integrated nature of the three areas isn't more explicit or explored. Via the diagram it is possible to see they are connected but at the 'horizon' and activity level this becomes lost, leaving the strategy feeling fragmented. An over-all vision statement incorporating all three themes might help.

It is via a document such as this that other needs-based initiatives should become apparent. I also support the establishment of a NZ Children's Laureate. Such a position has proven effective overseas in regards to advocacy, and would be relatively simple and quick to establish.

Which of the proposed 'A nation of readers' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Do you think the 3 broad themes outlined (One knowledge network, A nation of readers, Words as taonga) are the right areas of focus for the National Library looking towards 2030? 

Yes

Do the strategic directions fit with your view of the role of a national library?

Yes

Broadly, but I don't feel that 'Words as taonga' adequately describes the activities it stands for.

Redeveloped digital services for better interaction with National Library collections

To improve access to our collections. I would also like to add scaled up digitization as part of that too.

A cross-sector partnership to improve literacy levels

A cross-sector partnership to improve literacy levels

This can be problematic, especially stakeholders have different approaches.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'Words as taonga' do you consider to be most important?

Shared expertise to acquire, curate & preserve our documentary heritage & make it accessible

Why?

We have a unique, essential, trusted role to keep NZ's heritage safe so NZers can understand how they came to be what they are today and can apply lessons learned from the past to shape their future. It's really important that access to this knowledge is as equitable as possible. This may be a costly and therefore controversial project.

Which of the proposed 'Words as taonga' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

A state-of-the-art National Library space for discussion & debate

Why?

This may be a costly and therefore controversial project.

What do you think is missing from the National Library’s draft strategic directions?

I feel another strategic goal could be to inspire research and inquiry. Our National Library customers are also ‘inquirers’, from teachers and children using our resources in inspire inquiry in the classroom to researchers using our digital and physical records and resources to find information they need. This goal could connect the other 3 goals — inquirers need a path to find knowledge (One knowledge network), they need access to documentary heritage to learn from the past and inspire the future (Words as taonga), and good readers make good inquirers — and sometimes interest in a topic can motivate readers (A nation of readers). On a micro-level, adding inspiring research and inquiry as another strategic direction would also support Services to School’s lending model and work with digital literacy. Adding to this, inquiry resources also date quickly. Should we be also positioning ourselves as supporting/expanding schools’ non-fiction resource base to ensure their collections stay relevant to learning?
