Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'One knowledge network' do you consider to be most important?

Local government & schools taking part in a shared library system

Why?

So children are aware of learning resources that are available in the National Library. Opportunities of engagement for Kura Kaupapa Māori, Reo Rūmaki and Kohanga Reo. This is an area that needs lots of work if we are improve revitalisation of te reo Māori.

Which of the proposed 'One knowledge network' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Local government and schools taking part in a NZ-wide shared library system

Why?

Lacking resource capacitiy to Kura Kaupapa Māori, Reo Rūmaki and Kohanga Reo.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'A nation of readers' do you consider to be most important?

Distribute te reo material through e-devices to te reo Māori learners

Why?

Increase te reo Māori learning capacity, finding material using te reo Māori i.e. Māori Subject Headings especially tamariki.

Which of the proposed 'A nation of readers' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Distribute te reo material through e-devices to te reo Māori learners

Why?

Lack of budget to accommodate the reo Māori skills needed to provide this type of service; average price for translating services is very high.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'Words as taonga' do you consider to be most important?

Digitisation and distribution of te reo Māori published content

Why?

Looking at things with a Māori world view is very important, te reo Māori content provides a platform for future enhancement.

Which of the proposed 'Words as taonga' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Digitisation and distribution of te reo Māori published content

Why?

This area needs a dedicated team who specialise in skills around translation services and Mātauranga Māori concepts.

What do you think is missing from the National Library’s draft strategic directions?

Detail around implementation of Kaupapa Māori aspects especially the how it is going to get done and where the funding might come from.

Do you think the 3 broad themes outlined (One knowledge network, A nation of readers, Words as taonga) are the right areas of focus for the National Library looking towards 2030? 

Yes

Why or why not?

Yes but with some concerns. One knowledge network - I would hate to see schools, libraries and other institutions tied in to only one library management system or proprietary software - should be more focussed on standardised format for digital content that can be accessed by a variety of software, platforms - ie. z39.50 protocols, ePub format, MARC records etc. - whatever the prevailing standards are. Also I think there should be more focus on information literacy and digital literacy as well as reading literacy

Do the strategic directions fit with your view of the role of a national library?

Yes

Why or why not?

Most important role of National Library is the preservation of our documentary heritage. Digital access to collections and reading, information and digital literacy are also important so that people can get the most use and value from the materials collected

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'One knowledge network' do you consider to be most important?

Significantly scaled up digitisation of NZ content

Why?

Particularly relevant for communities outside of the main centres who do not have the same degree of access to physical collections

Which of the proposed 'One knowledge network' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Local government and schools taking part in a NZ-wide shared library system

Why?

Tied in to one propriety software

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'A nation of readers' do you consider to be most important?

Programmes to inspire reading & support literacy

Why?

This appears to be the idea most visible to people and communities and possibly the most fun way to improve literacy levels

Which of the proposed 'A nation of readers' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Cross-sector commitment for positive outcomes through reading

Why?

Always difficult reaching consensus with other sectors who may have different agendas

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'Words as taonga' do you consider to be most important?

Shared expertise to acquire, curate & preserve our documentary heritage & make it accessible

Why?

Important to have experience and expertise in assessing that the appropriate material is collected and preserved in formats that will last

Which of the proposed 'Words as taonga' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Why?

Not sure that this a significant role of the National Library

What sector do you work in?

Education

How often do you interact with the National Library of New Zealand?

Frequently

Do you think the 3 broad themes outlined (One knowledge network, A nation of readers, Words as taonga) are the right areas of focus for the National Library looking towards 2030? 

No

Why or why not?

Words are not taonga - they are code for ideas, ideas are not taonga they are socially and culturally constructed memes, memes are not taonga they flow like info, change like the wind and have no treasured associations. People are taonga, te reo may be taonga, cultural items ARE taonga when designated and recognized as such by Maori. The key problem for NatLib - apart from its relegation to DIA rather than being in Culture and Heritage or with te Papa, Nat Archives etc as "national institutions of record" - is its attachment to analogue, carbon based resources rather than digital. electronic records. The report trumpets 142TB well i have 20TB on desk more in the clouds so I suggest that the Nat Lib needs to move to the 21stC and digitize all its records, resources and responses. I was teh first to offer digital records to the NatLib and would like to build on that by preserving all my drives in sync to Nat Lib on an ongoing basis i suggest 1000 of these would be useful chosen either at random or better still by volunteers and patrons esp MPs they can be time embargoed but will prove useful.

Do the strategic directions fit with your view of the role of a national library?

No

Why or why not?

Bring back the targeted books, DVDs and records to school users not some basket of popular readers from the last millennium - kids neither want need nor intend to read your choice random term wise collections of books - they are way beyond that - and you need to be too Deliver all resources digitally and store off site out of wellington all physical items not on exhibition - until the next generation of digitization comes along - all digital items to teh cloud with creative commons with attribution to national library for all - stop collecting physical items only collect digital link up with weta and Te Papa (TWaT/Poneke? and Manukau) to display treaty - there is no need for you to waste vast quantities of space and resources doing this - Archives was more than adequate and Te Papa(s) National Services and a roadshow to all major museums 15+? would be the preferred option. I think Home is excellent and the best use of space in a long time, I love ATL as they are awesome collection staff and service wide - keep them and a small digitization team - retire the rest - digitize and downsize NatLib

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'One knowledge network' do you consider to be most important?

Significantly scaled up digitisation of NZ content

Why?

That is your only taonga, purpose and mission of value, the exhibitions are better at Te Papa, the treaty and record re better at Archives, split off ATL on Level 1 as its own library of research excellence - digitize all you have, store it safely - out of wellington - and retire. You have completely stuffed the national library delivery of RELEVANT TIMELY and USEFUL books to schools - we neither need nor want your packs of interested reading - kids just don't do that anymore - they catch creatures in augmented reality, design cat meme websites of tablets, film and star in their own youtube channels - none of which you have do or support - i think you are an artefact of information history and increasingly redundant - I would like access to all current scientific journals here and overseas through national library for free and it would be great to have all NZ items online - but apart from ATL I think you are reaching your used by date as an institution, meme developer, artform and archives - I mean i truly love your talks, presentation and Home is perfect but books are dead carbon IMHO

Which of the proposed 'One knowledge network' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Significantly scaled up digitisation of NZ content

Why?

You have 142 TB of data, 14% of NZ publications are online so to be 100% at the moment you need less than 2PB in 2016 but this is doubling exponentially annually so that is 4, 8, 16, 32PB by 2020 - and that doesn't include all the new data sources f the 5M NZers here and overseas. If you waste your moneym, time and resources on Treaty exhibitions when Archives or Te Papa(s) or National Services roadshow could do a far better cheaper and apposite job then you will never fulfill your key mission to record, preserve and make available NZers published output. Set ATL up with a putea of money and give them as many floors at NatLib as they need and digitize all non historic books and all historic works and store all records away from wellington in a safe neutral place (PN?) till they need to be redigitized or holoized. Unless the staffs output relates to digitization or web-based delivery reach a mutually agreeable severance package and devote all money to digitization. You cant do both - vainglorious politically inspired ToW shows and digitizing national collections - return Tow to Archives or cede to Te Papa

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'A nation of readers' do you consider to be most important?

A cross-sector partnership to improve literacy levels

Why?

This is not, never was and never will be your job, mission or goal, National Library has neither the teachers, literacy experts, access, time resources nor money to do this. It stems from you having books and wanting people to read them - well get with the programme, digitize them and walk away. I f you want to deaccession offer your carbon based out of date redundant texts to local and school libraries but i think you will find they too can't bear the cost of upkeep, collection management and curation. Digitize, store or abandon all carbon based texts, resources and publications (UNLESS they are ATL unique collections and manuscripts and incidentally shouldn't they choose to de-accession and sell on open market or to collectors local - national or international non NZ material which is held elsewhere - to get teh resources to survive on all floors of present building. teachers and educations, parents and communities do literacy and reading - you do recording, preserving and accessibility to NZ publications Literacy is not your problem - your issue is what to do with redundant forms, media and mountains of information based on analogue carbon storage mechanisms - answer digitize it.

Which of the proposed 'A nation of readers' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

Cross-sector commitment for positive outcomes through reading

Why?

Its a BS, apple pie and motherhood meaningless mess of ideas unrelated to your core outputs. We all agree its a great idea but your books are an archaic form of info technology - we need digital literacy on all devices - if your"books" ebooks films, images manuscripts whakapapa etc are there so much teh better but no -one wants needs or uses your books and those we did use in schools related to specific topics, subject and assessments have been replaced by semiannual random selections of what NatLib people think our students should could or will read - well they don't - they won't and the aren't. By the way return to supplying teh needs of teachers, students and school libraries if you ant to remain relevant to them let alone have them distribute your old cast off last millenniums publications no matter how worthy learned or well-thumbed. This nation of readers seems driven by political needs for relevance than real analysis, consultation or thought and is at best half-baked, at worse addled thinking. Give up on this dream - the time has past, the boat has sailed and we have lost reading as a pursuit, pastime and nation

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'Words as taonga' do you consider to be most important?

A sustainable, fit-for-purpose digital preservation approach to manage new digital content

Why?

Approach 1000 Nzers either randomly or targeted as patrons, politicians or personalities deliver all their digital records and outputs to you under creative commons with attribution and share-alike for the life of them and their descendants ceding all moral right and residual rights to you in perpetuity. I am happy to be the first so to offer - already i have 20Tb of documents, records, publications on my desk and 7000+ videos I have made in the clouds of Adobe, YouTube, Google, MS etc with over a million images and 7 million viewers. You are welcome to all I have make, produce and create - synchronize teh drives to your computer storage system - do this for the other 999 NZers you select or who volunteer (from on and off shore) Ask for and store in a synced format all publications, artworks, video, media, productions and documents records and databases of national importance, sample for regional, local, special interest groups, nationalities ages stages, group's culture societies etc. Think about direct feeds for all school work, assessments and correspondence from 1000 wellington based digital natives from 0 - 130 - based on the Dunedin longitudinal study - prepare for new media

Which of the proposed 'Words as taonga' ideas for success do you consider to be most problematic?

A state-of-the-art National Library space for discussion & debate

Why?

you have one - its called the ground floor - you also have the floor on te street level and once you have downsized to digitization team and the newly independent ATL you should have heaps of space - also all discussion and debate is already on the web at twitter, FB, Youtube, google, MS etc - and that is actually state-of the-art. I think the whole words as Taonga section is deeply flawed and shows a lack of understanding of both words as idea signifiers and taonga as treasured possessions. I think you need to move beyond words, languages and books to discussion and debate on line - so analogue to digital, physical to virtual, space to time online. Stop building warehouses of carbon, galleries for politicians and places for books - digitize all you have - store them in Palmerston North if needs be and move your data, discussions and debates online. I think the goals and motives of DIA is pushing your constitutional documents learning debate - that's Archives and Te Papa not your core business. i think MOE and MBIE is driving your literacy goal and that's a teachers, community, whanau responsibility and resource issue IMHO.

What do you think is missing from the National Library’s draft strategic directions?

Purpose, Mission, Clarity, Goals, Understanding, future focus, costings, direction, real world politics, freedom from DIA, MOE, MBIE and other ministerial yokes. You lack a clear understanding of THE role of the National Library to preserve, provide access and promote NZ based publications, record and archives. You lack clear boundaries re: Te Papa, National Archives, local and school libraries and attempt to move into National Exhibitions, archives & constitutional documents, and local lending book items. You seem to be trying to expand into others patches, areas of expertise and publications - stick to your knitting and get the other 86% of NZ publications online now - then double that digitization efforts annually and you will find you have neither the staff, energy nor resources to build new mausoleums, monuments or museums of words. Your ambitions are lofty, even over reaching but they do not adequately address your core mission of making NZ publications freely available (online for free with creative commons by attribution and sharealike status) Nothing in the above, present or future should detract in anyway from the excellent work, collection and service of ATL - while you have traded in your space for Home they have kept ahikaa alive.

How does the National Library’s draft strategic directions fit with your organisation’s priorities?

Your literacy Goals miss out digital literacy and you have neither teh resources, expertise or mandate to undertake it. Your Constitutional document exhibition undercuts Archives and Te Papa and would be better suited, managed and costed at either of those venues or on a user pays roadtrip to 15+MNZ "national" museums and Iwi 'nations' Marae nationwide starting with Waitangi and heading south following teh signing ceremonies dates where possible We at schools relied on your boxes of subject relevant, topic sensitive, assessment tuned books and publications every term for many many years and you have abandoned that for a grabbag of cast off fiction titles none of our kids will read and serious pointless to all schools and libraries - so i would start by reinstating a targeted service of books requested by school and marae to their kura, whare wananga, college, library or school. Your digitization of all NZ publications by calendar year end and maintenance of all NZ publications on an ongoing basis is useful as long as they are copied creative commons by attribution with sharealike and available on all devices, media and platforms in perpetuity for free. The 1000 person drive synchronization and storage is good.

In which areas are you or your organisation interested in partnering with or contributing to the National Library?

I hereby bequeath and give unreservedly forever all moral, legal, residual and copyright (based on creative commons by attribution with sharealike for all correspondence, images, publications documents and items of communication in all medias to teh National Library and its proceding organizations - to be synced in real time, held digitally and discovered by any living or unliving life form, AI, database forever. ie total rights and control to National library. I suggest they do this with a trial group of 1000 NZers either randomly chosen, school based (with parental permission) or chosen by whichever criteria they wish with either time embargoed, privacy redacted or commercial sensitivity limits as negotiated on a case by case basis. National Library can have all my drives, books, records, archives, publications on my death or in stages before that if they so wish if they are looking for relevant digital archives or predigtalized records. We will gratefully accept reinstatement of the targeted teacher requested school library mediated, books, videos and publications from the National library as soon as possible and the rejection of the grabbag fiction based reading boxes of books we currently have which is of less use, utility and worth to us.

How would you like to be involved as the strategic directions are progressed?

As a potential partner

Any other thoughts you'd like to share?

The National library has great coffee, cakes and salads but alas that is what a National Cafe might feature - it has no books that i can see and a walled up Cliff Whiting - it strives to be teh new Archives display space and to get all kids reading which while laudatory aims is not its core or even peripheral business. The National Library's Mission is to preserve, promote and ponder NZers local and national publications and to provide digital online free access with Creative commons (attribution and sharealike) to them. Accordingly while setting up entrusting and housing in perpetuity the ATL it needs to downsize all of its departments to get access to the ongoing resources required to digitize its collections then upload them free and CC to its freely accessible and downloadable websites. Any spare resources, space or time could go into digital innovation and creativity in the redesign rebuild and recreation o ATL on all present floors of teh National Library Building. As each publication has been batch digitized they can be stored off site (PN?) waiting in climate controlled conditions till the next generation of digitization requires their short term return for new format copying.

Basically, lack of interest from the public.

Do you think the 3 broad themes outlined (One knowledge network, A nation of readers, Words as taonga) are the right areas of focus for the National Library looking towards 2030?

(1) For me, the primary disruptive technology is digital. The themes don't explicitly identify digital as both a threat and an opportunity for the National Library going forward. Tackling the direct implications of digital head on as primary imperative for a knowledge organisation feels like the elephant in the room. (2) To me the core themes distill a sense of bookishness, or a preponderance with books that diminishes other informational sources.

Do the strategic directions fit with your view of the role of a national library?

No

Why or why not?

OKN seems relatively useful, however its unclear how this theme is different to the work already undertaken under the DNZ bus. group. Words as Taonga, for me, misses the breadth and depth of the collections we have, and we intend to collect going forward. Nation of readers seems like an issue for Min. Ed, MBIE and public libraries. Whilst the nat lib ought to be involved in the concept, it feels markedly cross departmental, and as such somewhat misplaced as one of three identified strands of core work.

Which of the proposed ideas for success for 'One knowledge network' do you consider to be most important?

Redeveloped digital services for better interaction with National Library collections

Why?

Much of the NLNZ collection is currently under utilised. We can add value to the nations cultural assets by providing insightful and feature rich access to collections.

Local government and schools taking part in a NZ-wide shared library system

This seems like an issue for govt. procurement, not nat lib. If I was a public lib I would also see this as a territory incursion.

Seems like its in line with the Nat lib Poet Laureate.
