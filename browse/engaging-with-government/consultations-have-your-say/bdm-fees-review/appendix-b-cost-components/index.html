Appendix B: Cost components

Cost structures and drivers

All Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) products and services are managed through the Issue of Birth, Death and Marriage Certifications and other Products and the Marriage and Civil Union Products Memorandum Accounts under a common cost structure. The Department of Internal Affairs’s (the Department’s) cost structure and drivers were reviewed independently by PWC in 2015 during the development of the pricing model to support the projection of future operating costs.

Volumes and product channels were identified as the most significant cost drivers that directly impact on the unit costs. It should be noted that:

  • The volume of products can vary from less than 100 products sought per year, to more than 200,000;
  • Customers may seek to access a product and/or service through a range of channels (i.e. through an online service, at a public counter, via a contact centre request, or through postal requests). The method of access impacts on the cost to produce a product or service;
  • Changes in customer behaviour linked to the availability of online services impacts on the projected future cost of products and services. As more products and services are offered online, the cost of overheads may reduce; and
  • Assumptions regarding the impact of changes in fees for a product or service and the future demand for that product or service are outlined in Appendix C.

Assumptions relating to the time taken to produce an individual product or service, the current channel by which a customer seeks to access that product and service, along with anticipated changes to these variables in the future have been considered with regard to each of the fees outlined in Appendix A.

Key terms

The key cost components attached to volumes relate to direct costs, indirect costs and business support costs. These terms are used to specify costs in tables 16-23. Details are outlined below.

Direct costs

This consists of staff directly involved in the production of certificates and other products. Staff requirements have been arrived at by estimating the process time required to complete each activity in the process. These process times vary based on product type and product channel (online or paper). Direct labour cost is directly influenced by the volumes of demand over the period.


Operating costs include all costs influenced by volumes and product channels such as the cost of certificate and decorative paper, contact centre and direct customer service costs, and costs payable to other agencies based on volumes. Other agencies include the Ministry of Justice who deliver marriage related services on the Department’s behalf.

Indirect costs

These are all other non-volume related costs such as:

Indirect technology – cost of providing technology;

Indirect asset related –- depreciation and capital charge on the assets; with the increased investment around technology upgrades and other enhancements, asset related costs such as depreciation and capital charge will increase.

The Indirect – other includes the costs related to the management of BDM operations and Customer Services. It also includes internal branch development and support costs. These include costs relating to storage, telephony, travel, consumables, fraud assurance, training and knowledge management and corporate management.

Business support cost

This includes a share of the Department’s operating costs such as Finance, Human Resources and Strategy and Planning.
