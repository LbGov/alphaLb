Executive summary

Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) within the Department of Internal Affairs (the Department) is responsible for the registration of life events that take place in New Zealand under the Births, Deaths, Marriages, and Relationship Registrations Act (BDMRRA) 1995. The events that are registered include births, adoptions, gender reassignments, registered name changes, marriages, civil unions and deaths. BDM has custodial responsibilities concerning records of these events and access to them.

Funding of BDM products and services is split between public (Crown-funded) and private good (funded through fees). Crown funding covers the cost of the registration of life events in New Zealand. It does not cover, nor subsidise the cost of providing information and documents to customers. It has been 13 years since the Department made adjustments to product and service fees. The current fees were introduced in September 2003 (or, in the case of the civil unions, 2005).

Increased costs

While the price of BDM products and services has remained stable, the cost of providing them has increased over time. The Department has absorbed these increases through improved efficiency and savings rather than by seeking to increase fees.

Business changes have been made to increase efficiency and effectiveness. Online tools have been introduced for birth notification and registration, as well as online and phone orders for certificates and printouts. Unproductive systems and processes have been eliminated, and service developments have enabled customers to resolve issues without contacting the Department directly.

The savings made from improved production processes have been moderated by the implementation of compulsory identity verification requirements.

However, after 13 years of maintaining the current fees, the cost of providing products and services is now more than the revenue received. This means that the Department’s Memorandum accounts1 which are used to manage the costs and income from these services have fallen into deficit. The forecast deficit is not sustainable and means that the Department is limited in its ability to introduce new and improved methods of delivering services to customers.

The current and projected under-recovery of costs is reflected in the growing deficit in the balance of the memorandum accounts. At the end of the 2015/16 financial year, the Issue of Birth, Death and Marriage Certifications and other Products (BDM Other) memorandum account was in deficit by $1.148 million. The Marriage and Civil Union Products memorandum account balance was in deficit of $0.716 million.

Proposed new fees

It is therefore proposed to set new fees to address the gap between fee revenue and service delivery costs, reduce the memorandum accounts deficit over time, and ensure that the price of products and services more accurately reflects the cost of their provision.

Two BDM products, Paternity Search certificates and Original Birth (adoption) certificates have been deliberately excluded from this review. Each product is priced below cost as they serve as a public good, have very low order volumes, and make an insignificant financial impact overall.

Revenue from the sale of marriage and civil union licences is attributed to a specific memorandum account. This account also incurs the cost of the initial appointment and annual renewal of marriage and civil union celebrants. Although legislative provisions exist for a fee to be charged for the services the Department provides to celebrants, no fee is currently charged.

This means that couples entering into a marriage or civil union currently cover the market entry fees for celebrants. The rationale of whether this is a justified approach will be dependent on whether the couple is required to pay a fee to the celebrant for their services. This largely depends on the category of celebrant the couple choose and is discussed in more detail later in this document. 

To ensure that celebrants pay for the services that they receive from the Department, it is proposed to introduce a fee for the initial appointment, and annual renewal of independent and organisational celebrants. This will not apply to celebrants of specified religious bodies (listed in section 8 of the Marriage Act 1955). Celebrants of specified religious bodies are appointed under a different process requiring minimal effort from the Department.

The main benefits of the proposed changes are to create better equity among all customers, ensuring they pay for the product or service that they receive and where possible, at a rate that reflects the cost incurred by the Department.

Options

Three pricing options have been identified:

  • Status quo – where there is no change to current BDM fees;
  • Option one – an equal increase on fees and the introduction of celebrant fees to cover costs and reduce the memorandum account deficits over time; and
  • Option two – an increase in fees with a simplified fee structure enabling similar products and services to have identical pricing and the introduction of fees for celebrants.

The Department’s preferred pricing option is Option two.

Your views on the proposed changes to fees and alternate options are sought by 18 November 2016.
