  • Table 23 shows the forecast costs for producing copies of source documents, printouts and verifications.Â 
  • Almost 29,000 products in this group are produced annually.Â  Printout volumes account for 96% of the total volume. A printout is a copy of a registration record for a birth, death, marriage, civil union or name change.Â 
  • The proposed fee under Option two for this group is $21.74 (GST Excl.)
  • The Department has set the proposed fee below cost for the following reasons:
    • The information on a printout is a photocopy predominantly used for family research purposes whereas a certificate, which has several security features including the Registrar-Generalâ€™s seal, is primarily used for evidentiary purposes.
    • Printout customers tend to be returning customers, often ordering multiple printouts for genealogy purposes.
    • In most cases, evidence of identity is not required for printout requests because the requests are generally for historical records.1

Footnotes

1. The Births, Deaths, Marriages and Relationship Registration Act 1995 defines historical records information as:

  • births that occurred at least 100 years ago
  • stillbirths that occurred at least 50 years ago
  • marriages that occurred at least 80 years ago
  • civil unions that occurred at least 80 years ago
  • changes of name for a person whose birth is registered outside of New Zealand and who was born more than 100 years ago
  • deaths that occurred more than 50 years ago, or less than 50 years ago if more than 80 years has passed since the deceased person's birth.
