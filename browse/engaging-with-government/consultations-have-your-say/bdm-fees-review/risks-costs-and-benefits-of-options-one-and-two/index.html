Risks, costs and benefits of options one and two

In modelling the level of fee increases under these options, the Department of Internal Affairs (the Department) has conservatively assumed that:

  • there will be no significant rise or fall for demand in Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) products over the life of this fee;
  • digitisation of historical records removes the need for multiple fees for the product ‘printout’, as similar levels of effort are required regardless of the age of the record;
  • the number of independent and approved organisational celebrants will decrease by up to 10 per cent, as a result of registration and renewal fees;
  • the number of celebrants of specified religious bodies (SRBs) will remain unchanged, as they will be unaffected by the change in fees; and
  • the volume of applications for a marriage or civil union licence will not significantly rise or fall as a result of a fee increase.

It is possible that demand for some products or services will decrease as a result of fee increases. However, the rise in fees is not deemed to be of such an extent that demand will change. The impact of the fee increases is anticipated to be minimal for most customer groups.  However customers on low incomes are likely to be impacted.

These fees will enable the Department to recover the costs of providing products and services in a way that facilitates compliance with Treasury and Office of the Auditor-General (OAG) guidelines.

The Department considers that the fee increases proposed under both Options one and two strike a reasonable balance in attempting to fairly cover costs and in reducing the memorandum account deficits over the medium term. If the fees are set too high, there is a risk that the people of New Zealand will be unable to access important information relating to their life events and identity.  If set too low, the taxpayer will need to subsidise services or service standards will drop.

It is noted that under Option one, the BDM Other memorandum account does not reach a positive balance.  The rate of increase set for Option one, 27 per cent, ensures the BDM Other account deficit is reduced to a level the Department is comfortable with and the Marriages account is not left with a rapidly growing surplus. However, the individual fees will not accurately reflect the differences in the costs for producing each individual product (unlike Option two).

For these reasons, the Department’s preferred approach is Option two, as it simplifies the pricing structure for customers and enables some products to be priced so that they are affordable to all customers.

The Department would carefully monitor the financial situation and, if necessary, bring forward its planned 2019/20 review of fees if levels need to be adjusted.
