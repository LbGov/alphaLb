Principles for assessing fee options

The Department of Internal Affairs (the Department) undertook the review of fees with the objectives of:

  • meeting the Department’s costs in the production and supply of Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM) products and services;
  • meeting the Department’s costs in maintaining a register of celebrants;
  • reducing the deficit in the memorandum accounts between the time of a fee increase and the next review in 2019/20;
  • improving efficiency by increasing incentives on celebrants to register and renew only if they are active in officiating marriages and civil unions; and
  • ensuring costs correctly fall on those using the services e.g. those using independent celebrants or choosing to access other services or products.

The Treasury and the Office of the Auditor-General (OAG) list a number of principles that should be considered when setting fees and charges. This review has been undertaken with these principles in mind.1 The OAG notes that authority, efficiency and accountability are the principles to be incorporated in fee-setting decisions. The Treasury guidelines focus on efficient resource allocation, minimising transaction costs, achieving equity among taxpayers that give rise to costs, and reducing costs and the taxpayer burden.

The following criteria are used to assess the options against objectives:

  • recovery of the Department’s operating costs and elimination of the memorandum account deficit prior to the next review period;
  • those seeking BDM products and services cover the costs of their production; and
  • incentives around registration as a celebrant are such that only those likely to officiate at ceremonies apply.

Some products, such as change of name applications and certificates of no impediment, have fees set below cost because the proposed new fees already represent an increase of 40 per cent (with rounding to nearest $5). Despite the fact the products still cost more than the proposed fee, the Department believes it would not be desirable to increase these further in one step. Fees will be reviewed again in 2019/20 in line with Treasury guidance.

These products have had fees set lower than the cost to produce due to:

  • The average fee (excluding GST) was limited to a maximum of 35%-40% with $5 rounding compared to the previous fee;
  • The small volumes involved therefore full cost to produce is difficult to spread to the customers;
  • “Initial applicant for registration as a celebrant” is a new fee and also has small volumes; and
  • Within “Copies, printouts and verifications” volumes are heavily weighted to printouts (25,417) and the other two items account for a much smaller volume (1,373) but have higher cost to produce.
