Fees cover a variety of costs, driven by a multitude of activities undertaken by the Department of Internal Affairs (the Department). These include the time and resources taken to process an application for a product, identity verification activities, assessment of entitlement to obtain a product or service, and enhancements to service delivery (such as new channels and methods). They do not cover mandatory registration (such as for births and deaths), corrections of errors of fact, and the maintenance of registers. These functions are covered by Crown funding.

These fees have not been changed since 2003 (or, in the case of the civil unions, 2005). The Department has reviewed the current fees using a model developed by PWC and concluded that the revenue derived from them is not sufficient to cover the current and forecast costs incurred in providing products and services.

The Department therefore proposes to increase most fees in 2017 to address the funding issues identified and intends to review the fees again in 2019/20
