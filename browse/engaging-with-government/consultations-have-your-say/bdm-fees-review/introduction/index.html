Introduction

The Department of Internal Affairs (the Department) is seeking your views on proposed changes to fees for Births, Deaths and Marriages (BDM)  products and services under the Births, Deaths, Marriages, and Relationship Registrations Act (BDMRRA) 1995 and the Marriage Act 1955.

The purpose of the BDMRRA is to:

  • require the recording and verification of information relating to births, deaths, marriages, civil unions, name changes, adoption, and sexual assignments and reassignments, so as to provide –
    • a source of demographic information, and information about health, mortality, and other matters important for government;
    • an official record of births, deaths, marriages, civil unions, and name changes that can be used as evidence of those events and of age, identity, descent, whakapapa, and New Zealand citizenship
  • regulate access to, and disclosure of, information recorded in respect of these matters; and
  • regulate the provision and effect of certificates relating to information recorded in respect of births, deaths, marriage, civil unions and name changes.

The Marriage Act 1955 and Civil Union Act 2004 do not contain an explicit purpose statement. However, they allow for eligible couples to enter into a marriage or civil union, and for an official record of this to be made and kept by the Registrar-General, with appropriate disclosure and access provisions.

In order to achieve these purposes, the Department undertakes a registration function, covered by Crown funding. This amounted to $3.9 million per annum (around 25% of total BDM revenue) in 2015/16. The Department also provides information and documentation to New Zealanders. Products and services provided to customers are funded through the fees paid when the customer obtains the product or service. The range of customers is broad and involves all New Zealanders over time, as well as organisations, government agencies and interest groups. This amounted to $11.1 million in 2015/16 (or 75 per cent of total BDM revenue).
