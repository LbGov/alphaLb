Making a complaint

If you need to make a complaint, first contact the service provider or the department responsible. Most departments or agencies have special phone numbers or email addresses for complaints. If you're not sure, call the head office phone number for the department.

Consumer rights and complaints

If your concerns aren't responded to, let us know and we'll do our best to find the right contacts for you.

Contacting Govt.nz

If you can't work out who to get in touch with, email us and we'll do our best to find you the right contact. We'll reply within 5 working days — or sooner if we can.

Our working hours are 9am to 5pm, Monday to Friday.

Email: govtnz@dia.govt.nz

Our social media rules

If you send us questions, we'll do our best to answer them.

We want to be as useful as possible to everyone — as part of this we expect you, our users, to be as courteous to us as we will be to you.

If your message is abusive in any way, we may not respond to you and we might block you. We don’t tolerate abuse of any sort. This includes (but is not limited to) racism, sexism, bad language and threatening behaviour.

Our social media accounts are there to help us all talk to each other. They're not there for political purposes — this means we can’t respond to any political messages you send us.
